# SDR Organizational Hierarchy Framework

The agency structure are the enforcement bodies built by the department system. 

## Structural Hierarchy (Largest to Smallest)

1. **Agency**

 - **Scope:** Largest operational unit

 - **Function:** Manages vast, complex missions resembling government-within-government

 - **Characteristics:** Controls significant infrastructure, budget, and personnel across multiple major programs

2. **Administration**

 - **Scope:** Large, focused operational unit

 - **Function:** Direct management and delivery of critical nationwide systems

 - **Characteristics:** Primary implementer and enforcer for specific high-priority public functions

3. **Commission**

 - **Scope:** Mid-sized independent oversight body

 - **Function:** Regulation, adjudication, and deep investigation

 - **Characteristics:** Quasi-legislative and judicial powers; insulated from political cycles

4. **Division**

 - **Scope:** Core component of a Department

 - **Function:** Groups related policy areas and operational units

 - **Characteristics:** Thematic organization within larger departments

5. **Bureau**

 - **Scope:** Specialized unit within Division or Department

 - **Function:** Focuses on specific narrow tasks

 - **Characteristics:** Policy development, legal counsel, public affairs, or technical functions

6. **Section**

 - **Scope:** Smallest operational cell

 - **Function:** Executes discrete, specific actions

 - **Characteristics:** Team-based focus on single processes or locations

## Guiding Principle

> The scale of the organization must match the scope of its constitutional mandate, ensuring both efficiency and clarity of purpose across all levels of governance.
