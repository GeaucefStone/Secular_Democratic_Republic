# Tax Structure

## Overview
The Secular Democratic Republic employs a streamlined, transparent tax system designed to fund essential public services while maintaining economic fairness and sustainability. All tax revenues are constitutionally earmarked for specific public purposes.

## Tax Categories & Allocation

### 1. Property Tax

- **Purpose:** Serves as a public equity contribution for housing access

- **Rate:** Progressive based on property value and usage

- **Allocation:**
 * Public Housing Trust maintenance and expansion (60%)
 * Local infrastructure and utilities (40%)

- **Exemptions** (per Article III, Section 1):
 * Retired and disabled individuals
 * Legally emancipated minors under age 18
 * Self-sustaining property owners below income threshold
 * Full-time students regardless of age

### 2. Fuel and Wattage Tax

- **Scope:** All vehicle propulsion sources including:
 * Gasoline and diesel fuels
 * Electric vehicle charging
 * Hydrogen and alternative fuels

- **Note:** The tax rate for electricity (per kWh) is calibrated to be functionally equivalent to the tax rate on gasoline (per gallon), ensuring drivers contribute equally to infrastructure regardless of power source.

- **Allocation:**
 * Road construction and maintenance (70%)
 * Public transportation infrastructure (30%)

### 3. Income Tax

- **Structure:** Progressive marginal rates
 * 0-25,000 units: 0%
 * 25,001-75,000 units: 10%
 * 75,001-150,000 units: 20%
 * 150,001+ units: 30%

- **Allocation:**
 * Emergency services (police, fire, rescue): 40%
 * Public healthcare system: 60%

- **Features**: Automatic inflation adjustment of brackets

### 4. Sales Tax

- **Rate Structure:** A multi-tiered system applied to food and beverage transactions to promote public health.

- **Tier 1: Zero Tax (Essential & Healthy Foods)**
 * Basic, unprepared food items: Fresh fruits, fresh vegetables, legumes, grains, eggs, milk, plain yogurt, unflavored plant-based milk, meat, poultry, fish
 * Basic ingredients: Flour, cooking oils, spices, salt, yeast

- **Tier 2: Standard Tax (5%)**
 * Processed foods: Canned and frozen fruits/vegetables (with no added sugars/salts), bread, cheese, pasta, sauces, nut butters (no added sugar)
 * Beverages: Bottled water, unsweetened tea and coffee
 * All non-food commercial transactions

- **Tier 3: Surcharge (15-20%) (Junk Food & Sugary Drinks)**
 * Sugar-Sweetened Beverages: Sodas, energy drinks, sweetened juices, and flavored milk with added sugar
 * High-Sugar/High-Fat Snacks: Candy, chocolate, cookies, cakes, ice cream, potato chips, and other "discretionary" junk foods
 * Prepared, ultra-processed foods: Ready-meals high in sodium, sugar, or saturated fat

- **Allocation of Surcharge Revenue:**
 * Public health education and nutrition programs (50%)
 * Subsidies for fresh produce in low-income communities (30%)
 * Public healthcare system (20%)

## Special Provisions

### Tax Transparency

- All citizens receive an annual "Tax Impact Statement" showing exactly how their contributions were allocated

- Public dashboard available showing real-time tax collection and expenditure

### Anti-Evasion Measures

- Doctrine of Substance Over Form (Article II, Section 12) applies to all tax matters

- Enhanced penalties for tax evasion equivalent to underlying tax owed plus 100% penalty

### Local Option Taxes

- Municipalities may levy additional local sales taxes up to 2% with Citizen Assembly approval

- Local property tax surcharges permitted for specific local projects with 60% voter approval

## Implementation

This tax structure shall be administered by the Department of Economic Affairs and Labor, with oversight from the Citizens' Assembly to ensure compliance with constitutional principles of equity and transparency. Food categorization shall be determined by the Scientific Branch based on nutritional evidence.
