# Act for Nutritional Integrity in Public Education

**Act Number:** ACT-0011  
**Sponsoring Department:** Department of Public Health & Education  
**Co-Sponsors:** Scientific Branch, Worker's Branch  
**Date of Introduction:** [Date]

---

## **Preamble**

**WHEREAS** the health and well-being of students is essential to their educational development and long-term flourishing;

**WHEREAS** public schools serve as custodians of student health during school hours and have a duty to provide meals that meet evidence-based nutritional standards;

**WHEREAS** excessive consumption of processed sugars, artificial additives, and nutritionally deficient foods is linked to preventable health conditions, including metabolic disorders, dental decay, and impaired cognitive function;

**WHEREAS** the right of parents and guardians to provide personalized meals for their children is a fundamental exercise of familial autonomy and cultural practice;

**WHEREAS** Article III, Section 9 of the Constitution commits the state to ensuring the needs of every child are met;

**BE IT ENACTED** by the Legislative Congress of the Secular Democratic Republic as follows:

---

## **Section 1: Short Title**

This Act may be cited as the **“School Nutrition Integrity Act.”**

## **Section 2: Definitions**

- **“Public School”** means any educational institution from early childhood through secondary level that is funded wholly or in part by the state.
- **“School Meal”** means any food or beverage offered, sold, or provided to students by the school or its contracted vendors during school hours or at school-sponsored events.
- **“Artificial Additive”** includes artificial sweeteners, colors, flavors, preservatives, and synthetic emulsifiers not derived from natural sources.
- **“Personalized Meal”** means any food or beverage prepared at home or off-site by a student’s family or guardian for personal consumption.

## **Section 3: Prohibited Ingredients in School Meals**

1. **Added Sugars:** No school meal shall contain more than **5 grams of added sugar per serving**. Naturally occurring sugars in whole fruits, vegetables, and dairy are exempt.
2. **Artificial Additives:** The following are prohibited:
   - Artificial sweeteners (e.g., aspartame, sucralose)
   - Artificial colors (FD&C Red #40, Yellow #5, etc.)
   - Artificial flavors
   - Synthetic preservatives (e.g., BHA, BHT, sodium benzoate in excess of 0.1%)
   - Trans fats (partially hydrogenated oils)
3. **Highly Processed Ingredients:** Foods containing high-fructose corn syrup, mechanically separated meats, or protein isolates not derived from whole foods are prohibited.

## **Section 4: Nutritional Standards for School Meals**

1. **Whole Foods Emphasis:** At least **75%** of ingredients in school meals must be whole or minimally processed (e.g., whole grains, fresh or frozen vegetables, legumes, lean meats).
2. **Sodium Limits:** Sodium content shall not exceed **300mg per meal for elementary students** and **400mg for secondary students**.
3. **Fiber Requirement:** Each meal must contain at least **5 grams of dietary fiber**.
4. **Hydration:** Free, filtered water must be available at all meal times. Sugared beverages, including flavored milk, are prohibited.

## **Section 5: Exemption for Personalized Meals**

1. **Personalized meals brought from home are exempt from all restrictions in Sections 3 and 4.**
2. Schools shall not inspect, restrict, or penalize students for the content of personalized meals, except in cases of **documented severe allergies** where cross-contamination poses an immediate health risk.
3. Schools shall provide adequate refrigeration, heating, and eating facilities for students bringing personalized meals.

## **Section 6: Implementation & Compliance**

1. The Department of Public Health & Education shall publish **Nutritional Guidelines for Public Schools** within **6 months** of this Act’s passage.
2. All public schools shall achieve full compliance within **2 years**.
3. Small or rural schools may apply for a **1-year extension** if infrastructure upgrades are required.

## **Section 7: Oversight & Enforcement**

1. The **Scientific Branch** shall review and approve all nutritional guidelines and updates.
2. The **Office of Nutritional Compliance (ONC)** is established under the Department of Public Health & Education to:
   - Conduct annual audits of school meal programs
   - Investigate complaints
   - Issue corrective orders and fines for non-compliance
3. Violations may result in:
   - First offense: Mandatory staff retraining and public notice
   - Second offense: Fine of up to 1% of the school’s annual meal program budget
   - Third offense: Suspension of food service license until compliance is achieved

## **Section 8: Funding for Transition**

1. The **Public Health Transition Fund** (Article III, Sec. 6) shall allocate grants to schools for:
   - Kitchen equipment upgrades
   - Staff training in whole-foods preparation
   - Procurement of local, sustainable ingredients
2. Schools in low-income areas shall receive prioritized funding.

## **Section 9: Public Transparency**

1. All school meal ingredients, nutritional data, and sourcing information shall be published online in machine-readable format.
2. Menus shall be posted publicly at least one month in advance.

## **Constitutional Compliance Justification**

- **Article I, Section 6 (Bodily Autonomy):** Supports students’ right to healthy bodily development and informed nutrition.
- **Article III, Section 9 (Child Welfare):** Directly fulfills the state’s duty to meet the needs of children.
- **Article IV, Section 8 (Scientific Branch):** Ensures nutritional standards are evidence-based.
- **Article XV (Education):** Aligns with the purpose of education to promote flourishing and critical capacity.

**Methodology:** This Act follows the established legislative pipeline: identifies a public health concern, grounds it in constitutional rights, creates clear rules with exceptions for personal liberty, establishes oversight, and provides funding for equitable implementation.
