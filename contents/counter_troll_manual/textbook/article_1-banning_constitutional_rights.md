## Constitutional Framework Analysis

### Rights Balance Principles
The firearms provision embodies:
- **Individual Right:** Clear protection of arms possession and bearing
- **Community Safety:** Recognition that rights have boundaries where they impact others
- **Proportional Regulation:** Narrowly tailored rules addressing specific risks
- **Anti-Abuse Protection:** Explicit prohibition of de facto bans through regulation

### Constitutional Tests for Regulations
Valid regulations must meet:
1. **Narrow Tailoring:** Address specific, documented safety risks
2. **Non-Prohibitive:** Cannot function as de facto total ban
3. **Public Safety Basis:** Grounded in actual risk evidence
4. **Rights Preservation:** Maintain core right to self-defense

### Comparative Rights Context
This approach aligns with:
- **International Standards:** Most democracies balance rights with public safety
- **Legal Precedent:** Historical "time, place, and manner" restrictions on rights
- **Practical Governance:** Workable frameworks for dense population centers
