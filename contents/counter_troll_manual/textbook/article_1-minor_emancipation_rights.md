## Constitutional Framework Analysis

### Child Protection Principles
The emancipation provision embodies:
- **Parental Primacy:** Family preservation as default position
- **State Parens Patriae:** State as protector of last resort for endangered children
- **Proportional Response:** Limited emancipation rather than full state custody
- **Due Process:** Judicial oversight and legal representation

### Safeguard Architecture
Multiple layers prevent abuse:
1. **Evidentiary Threshold:** "Clear and convincing evidence" of severe threat
2. **Judicial Gatekeeping:** Court approval required
3. **Limited Scope:** Residential and financial independence only
4. **Continued Obligations:** Education and other minor protections maintained

### International Child Rights Context
This approach aligns with:
- **UN Convention on Children's Rights:** Protection from abuse and neglect
- **Comparative Law:** Many democracies provide similar emergency emancipation
- **Child Development Research:** Recognizing adolescent capacity in safety decisions
