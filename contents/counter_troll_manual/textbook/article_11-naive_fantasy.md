## Constitutional Framework Analysis

### Behavioral Economics Foundation
The provision applies modern behavioral insights:
- **Certainty Over Severity:** Certain detection and consequence matters more than extreme punishment
- **Structural Incentives:** Makes compliance the rational self-interested choice
- **Transparency Mechanisms:** Removes secrecy that enables corruption
- **Multiple Enforcement Points:** Creates redundancy in oversight

### Comparative Anti-Corruption Models
This approach synthesizes best practices from:
- **Singapore's CPIB:** Certain enforcement and professional anti-corruption agency
- **Hong Kong's ICAC:** Independent commission with broad powers
- **Norwegian Transparency Laws:** Public official financial tracking
- **New Zealand's Integrity Systems:** Multiple overlapping accountability mechanisms

### Enforcement Architecture
The system operates through:
1. **Prevention:** Making corruption legally dangerous to attempt
2. **Detection:** Multiple reporting and tracking mechanisms
3. **Enforcement:** Severe, certain penalties for violations
4. **Deterrence:** Public awareness of the system's operation
